Overview

Large language models (LLMs) have revolutionized natural language processing (NLP), enabling advanced human-like text understanding and generation. However, deploying these models on edge devices such as smartphones, IoT sensors, and embedded systems remains a challenge due to high computational demands, memory constraints, and the diverse nature of edge hardware.

This ongoing research aims to develop AI systems that can organize, adapt, and optimize themselves dynamically to meet diverse user requirements while ensuring privacy and efficiency. By utilizing knowledge distillation, this project transfers the reasoning and language capabilities of a 14-billion-parameter teacher model (DeepSeek-R1-14B) into more compact student models (Qwen and LLaMA3). The goal is to close the performance gap between small and large-scale models while ensuring edge feasibility, reducing computational overhead without sacrificing accuracy.

Building on the vision of connected intelligence this project integrates GPT-driven automation for task-oriented communication, federated learning, and model coordination, enabling efficient AI orchestration across edge devices. Through comparative experiments, we evaluate the accuracy of distilled models before and after training, while also assessing practical edge deployment on low-power hardware.

Background & Motivation

Large-scale AI models have achieved impressive breakthroughs in natural language processing (NLP), code generation, and task automation, but they are often too large and resource-intensive for real-world edge applications. Existing approaches rely on cloud-based processing, which introduces concerns related to: Latency: Cloud inference leads to delays in real-time applications like autonomous systems, healthcare, and robotics. Privacy & Security: Sensitive user data must be sent to external servers, increasing the risk of breaches. Energy Efficiency: Continuous cloud communication consumes high bandwidth and energy, making it unsuitable for power-limited devices.
