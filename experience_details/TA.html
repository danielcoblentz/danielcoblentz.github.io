Overview

As a Teaching Assistant for Object-Oriented Programming I & II, I worked closely with both students and instructors to reinforce core programming concepts. My role focused on leading lab sessions, providing detailed feedback on assignments, creating supplemental learning resources, and offering one-on-one help during office hours. This experience strengthened my ability to communicate technical material clearly while reinforcing my own knowledge of software engineering fundamentals.

Responsibilities

In this role, I led weekly coding labs and review sessions for more than 70 students, helping reinforce key concepts such as object-oriented design, recursion, and data structures. I conducted code reviews on student projects and assignments, providing detailed feedback on debugging strategies, coding efficiency, and best practices. To further support learning, I developed supplementary study materials, including practice problems and step-by-step exam guides. I also held regular office hours, offering one-on-one support to help students strengthen their problem-solving approaches and build confidence in applying programming concepts.

Teaching Approach

My approach to teaching emphasizes clarity, patience, and breaking complex ideas into manageable steps. For example, when introducing recursion, I often used visual examples like tree diagrams and compared it to "a function calling itself like a mirror reflection." This approach helped students understand abstract concepts in a more intuitive way.

Student Support

This position has helped me grow as both a mentor and a communicator. I’ve learned how to adapt explanations depending on the student’s background knowledge, and I’ve become better at identifying common misconceptions in programming. Seeing students go from “stuck” to “I got it!” moments has been one of the most rewarding parts of the role.

Outcomes & Reflections

Being a TA has helped me grow as a mentor and communicator. I’ve learned how to adapt my explanations to different levels of understanding and identify common misconceptions early. Seeing students progress from frustration to understanding has been one of the most rewarding aspects of the role. I plan to continue this position and expand the supplementary materials into a structured resource library for future classes.
